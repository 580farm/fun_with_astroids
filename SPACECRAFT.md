# Spacecraft Reference

This file tracks historically inspired spacecraft for future unlocks. Gameplay
stats do not need to be perfectly realistic, but each ship should feel connected
to its real role, era, and shape.

## Starter Candidates

| Spacecraft | Program or Country | Era | Gameplay Identity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mercury | United States | 1960s | Small, light, beginner-friendly capsule |
| Vostok | Soviet Union | 1960s | Stable capsule with forgiving handling |
| Voskhod | Soviet Union | 1960s | Heavier capsule, durable but less agile |
| Gemini | United States | 1960s | Better maneuvering and training focus |
| Apollo CSM | United States | 1960s-1970s | Balanced handling with strong upgrade path |
| Soyuz | Soviet Union / Russia | 1960s-present | Reliable, modular, good survival upgrades |
| Space Shuttle | United States | 1980s-2010s | Large profile, strong boost potential |
| Buran | Soviet Union | 1980s | Large profile, experimental upgrade path |
| Dragon | United States | 2010s-present | Modern capsule with strong automation theme |
| Orion | United States | 2010s-present | Heavy modern capsule with durability focus |
| Starliner | United States | 2010s-present | Modern capsule with balanced upgrades |
| New Shepard | United States | 2010s-present | Suborbital capsule, accessible early modern unlock |
| Starship | United States | 2020s-present | Large late-game vehicle or challenge unlock |

## Historical Fact Format

Each spacecraft can eventually use a short fact entry:

```text
Name:
First flight:
Country or program:
Mission role:
Fun fact:
Gameplay note:
```

## Research Guidelines

- Prefer official space agency pages, museum pages, and reputable educational
  sources.
- Keep facts short enough for kids to read between rounds.
- Distinguish real history from gameplay choices.
- When a vehicle is still changing, mark facts as needing review before release.
